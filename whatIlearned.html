Lecture 4

    Here's what I’ve learned about writing different types of letters:

    Formal Letters:
  • Greeting: Use "Dear Sir/Madam" or "Dear Mr/Mrs + surname."
  • Language: Use formal language with complex sentences and no abbreviations.
  • Structure: Include an introduction, main body (with separate paragraphs for each main point), and conclusion.
  • Ending: Use "Yours sincerely" (if the recipient’s name is known) or "Yours faithfully" (if not) followed by your full name.
    • Informal Letters:
  • Greeting: Use "Dear + first name" (e.g., "Dear kamal").
  • Language: Use informal language, idioms, colloquial English, and abbreviations.
  • Structure: Similar to formal letters but with a more casual tone.
  • Ending: Use "Love," "Yours," "Best wishes," or "Regards" followed by your first name.
    • Letters of Complaint:
  • Greeting: Use a formal greeting.
  • Introduction: State the reason for writing.
  • Main Body: Detail your complaints and provide justifications in separate paragraphs.
  • Conclusion: Suggest the action you want to be taken and close with a formal remark.
    • Letters of Apology:
  • Greeting: Can be formal or informal.
  • Introduction: State the reason for writing.
  • Main Body: Explain the reasons for the inconvenience caused.
  • Conclusion: Express regret or promise to make up for the situation.
    • Letters of Invitation:
  • Greeting: Can be formal or informal depending on the situation.
  • Introduction: State the reason for writing (to invite).
  • Main Body: Provide details about the event and directions if necessary.
  • Conclusion: Include closing remarks.
    • Accepting or Refusing Invitations:
  • Accepting: Thank the inviter and express pleasure in attending.
  • Refusing: Thank the inviter, explain why you cannot attend, and express regret.
    • Letters of Application (for a Job or Course):
  • Greeting: Use a formal greeting.
  • Introduction: State the reason for writing and mention where you saw the advertisement.
  • Main Body: Detail your qualifications, experience, and why you are suitable for the position or course.
  • Conclusion: Summarize your points and express interest in further communication.
    • Asking for/Giving Advice:
  • Asking for Advice: Explain your problem and ask for advice.
  • Giving Advice: Acknowledge the problem and provide suggestions with reasons.
    • General Tips:
  • Formal Letters: Use passive voice, avoid abbreviations, and use formal closings.
  • Informal Letters: Use a conversational tone, idiomatic expressions, and informal closings.
  • Structure: Always include an introduction, main body, and conclusion.
  • Linking Words: Use appropriate linking words and phrases to connect your ideas smoothly.

    Purpose: Emails are used for communication, both in personal and professional contexts

    Structure of the email(how to write it down correctly)

    Subject Line: Summary of the email’s content.

    Salutation: Greeting (e.g., “Dear kamal,” or “Hi vimal,”).

    Introduction: Briefly state the purpose of the email

    Main Content: Provide details, ask questions, or share information

    Closing: Summarize, express gratitude, or request action.

    Signature: Your name, job title, and contact information.
